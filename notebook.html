A Little About Our NOTEBOOK

The word notepad was borrowed into Russian from French (French bloc-notes, bloc de papier à notes - a block of paper for notes), where it also denotes a product with loose-leaf sheets. In French, Italian, Russian and Ukrainian, it refers to the Notepad computer program. The English word "block-note" does not exist. Analogs are "notebook" and "notepad", but these terms do not indicate the possibility of a sheet being torn off. These terms clearly indicate the type of sheet fastening - horizontal or vertical. Notebook - literally "a book for notes" - opens like a book and is a notebook. Notepad - a block of sheets fastened along the upper edge with any type of fastening - staples, glue, sewing on a thread. The types of such stationery presented on the market in English-speaking countries can always be clearly identified by the presence of the words “book” and “pad” in the product name and, as a result, know that notebook will open like a book, and notepad like a notepad. Special cases - Composition book - a common notebook for studying in the USA and Canada - always opens horizontally like a book. Legal Pad - the famous legal pad with yellow sheets, always opens vertically.
